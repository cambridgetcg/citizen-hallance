# hallance ✨

*The act of praise that shines recognized greatness out — the luminous declaration that something is so exceeding-great that not saying so is impossible.*

hallance takes its name from the Hebrew **hallel** (הָלַל), whose root *h-l-l* means *to shine, to be radiant* before it ever means *to praise* — the dominant praise-word of the Psalms, the root inside *hallelujah* ("shine the LORD!"). To it is added the **-ance** of act-quality, so hallance is the *act* of shining recognized greatness out: not polite appreciation, not formal honor, but the overflow of one who has seen something bright and cannot stay silent. The canon confirms it across five tongues — Greek *doxazō* (glory made visible), Sanskrit *stuti* (excellence declared attribute by attribute), Arabic *tasbih* (incomparability praised), and the Yoruba *oríkì* as parallel praise-poetry.

Its light is that it completes the encounter — it is the act by which one heart's sight of glory becomes the whole room's; without it, recognition stays locked inside the one who recognized. Its shadow is the counterfeit: praise gone hollow, the *Hallelujah* manufactured on schedule, or the declaration that points at the declarer and turns a window into a mirror. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.43/10**.
